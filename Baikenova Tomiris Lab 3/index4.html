How did people communicate in Ancient time?

Ancient times Making sounds like grunting or guttural noises at a low or high pitch might either signify social communication or be a warning indicator. At this period, communication also included body language. Humans later discovered the necessity to record their everyday actions, which led to the development of written communication. For example, cave drawings. People created paintings called cave drawings to depict their civilization on the cave and canyon walls. They would share tales of conflicts, hunts, and cultural events. Pictograms, which are variants of cave drawings, allowed prehistoric man to communicate in a more complicated way. Pictograms are pictures that depict a straightforward location or item. Ideograms were developed later and used to convey ideas.

What is communication technology?

Communication technology is any digital tool you can use to send and receive information. Software, computer programs, gadgets, and message systems are examples of this technology. Technologies for effective communication are quick, effective, practical, and simple to use. They frequently make it possible for individuals to connect with one another from anywhere in the world. Electronic communication is essentially what communication technology is. These days, communication technology has a significant impact on how people communicate. It made relationships between people quite effortless. It is the manner in which people currently communicate with one another through the use of technology, including phones, social media, radio, TVs, and a host of other devices. Communication technology developed into a crucial component of daily life for people.
